Do people still go to websites? Do people still… read? Seems like most people get information from headlines they don’t click, or from Instagram reels where people unnecessarily stand in front of their phone’s video camera, pointing at text, while feigning exasperation the way that people in informercials pretend to be incapable of functioning without whatever new revolutionary gizmo can be yours for four easy payments of $29.99. And I get that that’s preferable to what has become of the alternative, where half a paragraph or so of decently informative content has been fluffed up to 3,000 words by a content AI bot, to trick a search engine AI bot into thinking it's quality content, in order to attract more eyeballs (and AI bots) towards the obnoxious advertisements that completely cover up said content, preventing you from scrolling or clicking on it. Yeah, if that’s my other choice, I’ll take the guy with the weird YouTube voice that everyone learns from lord knows where, holding his wired earbuds’ little microphone, confidently explaining something he just learned an hour ago. “Did you know…?” And then he says something that’s like 60% true. And that’s the good part of the internet! To say nothing of the sea of ignorant lying assholes screaming in bad faith about drag queens or whatever else, at whichever innocent bystanders they’ve come to project all of their frustrations and increasingly terrifying feelings of impotence upon.

It’s all so loud.

I don’t know exactly what this is going to be yet, besides the new version of an old idea: To find the people out there that have (or will) inevitably come to suspect that they’re surrounded by bullshit, and to give them things to read about and think about, in a format that doesn’t disrespect their attention by commodifying it.
